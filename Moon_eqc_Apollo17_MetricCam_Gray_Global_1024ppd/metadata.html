Description:
Abstract:
The Apollo 17 DEM grayscale is generated from the Apollo 17 DEM data, by scaling elevation values from the range of -9135 to +8104 meters to integer values between 0 and 255. Elevation values below and above -9135 and 8104 meters are mapped to 0 and 255, respectively.
Purpose:
This grayscale image allows a visual interpretation of the Apollo 17 DEM.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
Precision data is provided at each pixel and is available for download. A visual representation of the precision data is available in the Confidence Map.
Logical_Consistency_Report:
The Apollo 17 DEM precison maps are generated using the elevation expected data standard deviation of all the available Apollo 17 DEM files. The precision values of the A17 DEM zone are captured in the file, Apollo17_MetricCam_Prec_Global_1024ppd.tif. The DEM confidence values are stored as 8-bit signed integer at 30 m/pixel resolution. The values in the confidence file are derived from the precision values. The value range from 10 to 14 represents poor confidence (10) to very good confidence (14), with 15 = manually edited. The confidence values in the range 10-14 represent values of the standard deviation stored in the precision files as follows: value 10 for standard deviation larger than 100m, value 11 for standard deviation in the range 75-100m, value 12 for standard deviation in the range 50-75m, value 13 for standard deviation in the range 25-50m, and value 14 for standard deviation in the range 0-25m.
Completeness_Report:
The accuracy and precision of the digital elevation map is lower in areas where the images where corrupted by noise in the camera path or scanning artifacts. While the effect of image noise is reduced by our robust stereo matching algorithm some of the artifacts remain present in the final product. Other areas of low precision and accuracy include shadow regions where stereo correlation has poor quality and areas along the image boundaries.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
Horizontal Accuracy is computed in terms of the standard deviation from a control network consisting of LOLA points. The mean horizontal plane error (two-dimensional Euclidean distance) to LOLA tracks is 170.7 meters and the estimated standard deviation is 95.7 meters.
Quantitative_Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Assessment:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Value: 170.7
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
Horizontal accuracy is computed using the Vison Workbench and Ames Stereo Pipeline packages. The error analysis for absolute horizontal position accuracy was computed during the bundle adjustment procedure which best aligns images to a limited set of LOLA ground control points. In addition, the horizontal accuracy was computed by investigating the horizontal distance between each LOLA point and the DEM post in a 400 x 400 meter vicinity that best matches the LOLA point altitude. This second method computes a more complete error measure by taking into account all available LOLA points over the imagery. Within the areas of high confidence characterized by precision levels in the range (0-50m) or confidence levels 13 and 14 (green and dark green in the confidence map) the horizontal accuracy is less than 100 meters. It is important to note that the inclusion of orbit terminator areas significantly reduces the average accuracy of the of the terrain model when compared to LOLA tracks. Until a LOLA control network becomes available, the accuracy results also include any potential errors in imprecise localization of the LOLA tracks.

Vertical Accuracy is calculated using Vision Workbench and Ames Stereo Pipeline packages. Vertical accuracy is computed as the standard deviation of the computed digital elevation values from the laser altimetry data where LOLA data is available. Within the areas of high confidence characterized by precision levels in the range (0-50m) or confidence levels 13 and 14 (green and dark green in the confidence map) with a coverage of minimum 40,000 square kilometers the vertical accuracy is less than 80 meters. It is important to note that the inclusion of orbit terminator areas significantly reduces the average accuracy of the terrain model when compared to LOLA tracks. Until a LOLA control network becomes available, the accuracy results also include any potential errors in imprecise localization of the LOLA tracks.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The Apollo 17 DEM is computed from stereo pairs (adjacent images within each orbit) of the A17 mission. The process uses the bundle adjustment solution and Bayesian subpixel correlator in Ames Stereo Pipeline package to reduce the uncertainties in camera position and reduce the effect of image noise. Elevation values extracted from overlapping stereo pairs (each point on the Lunar surface is seen by more than two images) are averaged and stored as the best elevation estimate. The standard deviation of all elevation values is used as a measure of precision.
