Title: Apollo 17 Metric Cam Image Mosaic
Description:
Abstract:
The Apollo 17 image mosaic is stored as 8-bit signed integer at 3033.3 ppd resolution. The invalid pixels are marked with 0 value. Value 255 is left unused. The mosaicking process includes an upsampling post processing step. The mosaic is generated using the Ames Stereo Pipeline: a software tool produced by the NASA Ames Intelligent Robotics Group (IRG) for automated processing of stereo imagery.
Purpose:
This Digital Image Mosaic provides a medium resolution representation of the equatorial regions of the Moon.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
Horizontal accuracy is computed using the Vison Workbench and Ames Stereo Pipeline packages. Horizontal Accuracy is computed in terms of the standard deviation from a control network of LOLA points. The horizontal errors were computed over the entire coverage of the mission. It is important to note that the inclusion of orbit terminator areas significantly reduces the average accuracy of the terrain model when compared to LOLA tracks. The accuracy results also include any potential errors in imprecise localization of the LOLA tracks.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The Apollo 17 Digital Image Mosaic is computed from nadir looking Apollo 17 images used for the computation of the Digital Elevation model. The original images are ortho-projected on the corresponding DEM and processed to correct variations in exposure time.
