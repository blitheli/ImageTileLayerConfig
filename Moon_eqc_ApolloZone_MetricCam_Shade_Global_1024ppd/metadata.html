Description:
Abstract:
The Apollo Zone DEM hillshade is generated using the hillshade function in Vision Workbench package. The DEM shade values are stored as 8-bit signed integer at 30m/pixel resolution. These DEM hillshade files were generated using the Vision Workbench library: a software tool produced by the NASA Ames Intelligent Robotics Group (IRG) for automated processing of stereo imagery.
Purpose:
This hillshade of Digital Elevation Model provides a medium resolution representation of the equatorial regions of the Moon.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
Precision data is provided at each pixel and is available for download. A visual representation of the precision data is available in the Confidence Map.
Logical_Consistency_Report:
The Apollo Zone DEM precison maps are generated using the elevation expected data standard deviation of all the available Apollo Zone DEM files. The DEM confidence maps represent at each pixel various labels and precision levels as described here: 0 = NoDATA, 1 = shadow, 2 = saturated, 3 = suspicious (edge, corner etc), 4 = interpolated/extrapolated (e.g. from neighbor pixels), 10 - 14 = value range of confidence derived from the precision maps (linear fit from poor 10 to very good 14), 15 = manually edited. The precision values of the Apollo Zone DEM are captured by the following files: apollo_Prec_1024PPD_4E_2N.tif ..apollo_Prec_1024PPD_180W_22S.tif
Completeness_Report:
The accuracy and precision of digital elevation map is lower in areas where the images where corrupted by noise in the camera path or scanning artifacts. While the effect of image noise is reduced by the our robust stereo matching algorithm some of the artifacts remain present in the final product. Other areas of low precision and accuracy include shadow regions where stereo correlation has poor quality and areas along the image boundaries.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
Horizontal Accuracy is computed in terms of the standard deviation from a control network consisting of LOLA points. The mean horizontal plane error (two-dimensional Euclidean distance) to LOLA tracks is 91.3 meters and the estimated standard deviation is 53 meters.
Quantitative_Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Assessment:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Value: 91.3
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
Horizontal accuracy is computed using the Vison Workbench and Ames Stereo Pipeline packages. The error analysis for absolute horizontal position accuracy was computed during the bundle adjustment procedure which best aligns images to a limited set of LOLA ground control points. In addition, the horizontal accuracy was computed by investigating the horizontal distance between each LOLA point and the DEM post in a 400 x 400 meter vicinity that best matches the LOLA point altitude. This second method computes a more complete error measure by taking into account all available LOLA points over the imagery. Within the areas of high confidence characterized by precision levels in the range (0-50m) or confidence levels 13 and 14 (green and dark green in the confidence map) the horizontal accuracy is less than 100 meters. It is important to note that the inclusion of orbit terminator areas significantly reduces the average accuracy of the of the terrain model when compared to LOLA tracks. Until a LOLA control network becomes available, the accuracy results also include any potential errors in imprecise localization of the LOLA tracks.
