Description:
Abstract:
This is a thematic color roughness map derived from the local roughness map LRO_NAC_Roughness_15m_25N311E_2mp.tif of Aristarchus 1. Roughness classification color ranges are arbitrary but representative of recent lander and rover designs, where the roughness scale is equivalent to the vehicle diameter of 15m.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Roughness data and thematic maps are derived from digital elevation models (DEM) and are essential for surface characterization and analysis, e.g., identifying potential landing sites, surface traversal planning, and landed asset placement.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
The roughness estimates and thematic maps are derived from the DEM for the Aristarchus 1 site.
Completeness_Report:
The DEM is geodetically controlled to LOLA and thus the derived roughness products are also controlled.
Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy of the roughness map is the same as the DEM from which the roughness map was derived. To compute the absolute horizontal accuracy, three of the sixteen LOLA tracks crossing the DEM were selected at random for comparison. The data set that the LOLA tracks were chosen from contains tracks that were not crossover corrected. Multiple tracks were sampled to account for this and ensure that offsets were consistent. The DEM was registered to these tracks to calculate the horizontal offset relative to LOLA and therefore find the absolute horizontal offset. The average horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, is 7.5 meters. The maximum horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, was 19 meters. Since the accuracy of the LOLA data is 20 meters, the maximum absolute horizontal offset for this site is 39 meters.
Quantitative_Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Assessment:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Value: 39
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is the same as the spatial resolution of the DEM which is 2m. The absolute horizontal accuracy is dependent upon the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data. When this DEM was made, the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data was 20 meters.
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy:
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute vertical accuracy of the roughness map is the same as the DEM from which it is derived. To compute the absolute horizontal accuracy three of the sixteen LOLA tracks crossing the DEM were selected at random for comparison. The data set that the LOLA tracks were chosen from contains tracks that were not crossover corrected. Multiple tracks were sampled to account for this and ensure that offsets were consistent. The DEM was registered to these tracks to calculate the elevation offset to LOLA and therefore find the absolute vertical error. The average elevation offset, relative to the LOLA data, is 2.02 meters. The maximum elevation offset, relative to the LOLA offset was 3.04 meters. Since the current elevation accuracy for LOLA data is 1 meter, the absolute elevation offset for this site is 4.04 meters.
Quantitative_Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Assessment:
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Value: 4.04
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
Since the DEM is tied to LOLA, the vertical accuracy is limited by both the absolute vertical accuracy of the LOLA data and the Expected Vertical Precision (the relative precision) of the DEM. The absolute vertical accuracy of the LOLA data is 1 meter. The relative precision of the DEM is expected to be 1 meter, but can be as large as 2 meters.

Process_Description:
Thematic roughness maps are generated using GDAL. The 5-color maps represent general purpose levels of local roughness in meters: blue [0.0-0.05], green [0.05-0.1], yellow [0.1-0.2], orange [0.2-0.3], graded red [0.3-2.0].
