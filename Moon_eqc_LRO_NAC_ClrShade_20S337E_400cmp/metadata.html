Description:
Abstract:
This is a colorized shaded relief of the digital elevation model (DEM) extracted from the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) images from the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter mission. The data product is a DEM from all NAC images that were acquired and made available via PDS at the time of the DEM construction. Images included are from both the Commisioning and the Mapping mission phases and vary from approaximately 2.m/p to 0.5 m/p in resolution at the image boresight. The DEM scale for this delivery is 4.0 m/pixel with a final delivery at 1.5 m/pixel and with a comparable horizontal resolution. The DEM was registered horizontally and vertically to LRO&apos;s Lunar Orbiter Laser Altimeter (LOLA) data. Elevation values are in meters and referred to a radius of 1737400m.
Purpose:
The NASA Constellation Program (CxP) has identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon that the LROC NAC has imaged. The DEMs are generated from these images. High resolution DEMs will be used for studying potential landing sites, analyzing landing site hazards, descent-phase navigation and surface operations. These DEMs will also be used for image ortho-projection and mosaics.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU). The nodata value for the shaded relief is 0.

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
For each DEM pixel, a derived confidence value is also assigned in the file named LRO_NAC_Conf_20S337E_400cmp.tif. The confidence values used to assess the DEM pixels are: 0 = NoDATA, outside boundary; 10-14 = Value range for confidence of topographic accuracy. A number of limitations and error sources are inherent with this method in strong correlation with the data set. Such error sources include: 1) The method computes heights by first estimating local slopes. To do so it ideally requires images at both nadir and off nadir angles and at varying solar azimuth and elevation angles. When there&apos;s a limiting range of solar elevation and azimuth angles the ability of the method to differentiate between albedo and slopes is limited accordingly. In the case of NAC images, typically we see that slopes along the flight path (latitude coordinate) are not estimated as well as slopes along the longitude coordinate. This may produce the effect of &quot;ripples&quot; appearing along the direction where the slopes are not as well estimated. 2) In areas overlapping the extent of a maplet where there is not a complete overlap of NAC images, but only partial contribution of images, discontinuities in the heights may appear along the latitude direction due to discontinuities in the slopes. This effect, where possible, is ameliorated, by including lower resolution NAC images, but it cannot be eliminated completely due to sparse coverage in certain areas. In this event discontinuities may appear along the latitude direction in the DEM. 3) The local horizontal and vertical position estimation does vary with the number of images used. Within the extent of a 10 km x 10 km area, the NAC coverage may vary from 2-8 images. As a result the relative position accuracy may vary slowly across the site, from ~1.5 m where there are more than 7 images to up to 3 meters where there are only 2 images. 4) Permanently dark areas, which can be present at high latitude regions or at deep craters cannot be modeled and appear as dark or no data in the DEM.

Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is comparable to the spatial resolution of the DEM. The absolute horizontal accuracy is dependent on the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data. The DTM was adjusted in its absolute horizontal position to the LOLA data (2011 March 31st PDS release) by cross-correlating to the highest resolution LOLA gridded DTM of that region, of approximately 30 m/pixel resolution. The final post-fit mean horizontal position error of the DEM relative to LOLA is less than 2 meters. The absolute horizontal position accuracy of LOLA from the crossover solutions is approximately 25 meters.

Vertical_Positional_Accuracy:
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The Expected Vertical Precision (the relative precision) of the DEM is comparable to it&apos;s horizontal resolution, in the 2-3 m range. The DEM was globally adjusted in height via cross-correlation with the LOLA gridded DTM of that region (2011 March 31st PDS release) to match the mean height of the LOLA DTM. The absolute vertical accuracy of the LOLA DTM with the crossover solutions is expected to be 1-2 meters. The mean residual height error relative to LOLA was less than 2 meters. The 1-sigma RMS height difference relative to the LOLA gridded DTM is 20 m. Therefore the overall absolute height accuracy of the DEM is expected to be 20 m.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Processing was performed using Geospatial Data Abstraction Library (GDAL) tools v 1.8 on the original LROC DEM. To derive this colorized shaded relief three GDAL commands were used: (1) gdaldem hillshade input_dem output_Shade.tif -z 2 (Z Factor); (2) gdaldem color-relief input_dem LMMP_color.lut output_colorTmp.tif; (3) hsv_merge.py output_colorTmp.tif output_Shade.tif output_ClrShade.tif. Pixels do not contain any of the original explicit elevation information. The colorized shaded relief represents approximate elevations based on the original topographic data. Elevations values are mapped based on the defined red, green, and blue mapping listed below. All NoData values are mapped to 0,0,0 (black). Thus 0 percent (purple) maps to minimum elevation and 100 percent (white) maps to maximum using a linear stretch between percentages. nv 0 0 0 //No Data; 0% 255 120 255 //purple; 20% 120 120 255 //blue; 40% 120 255 255 //aqua; 60% 120 255 120 //green; 70% 255 255 120 //yellow; 90% 255 120 120 //red; 100% 255 255 255 //white.
