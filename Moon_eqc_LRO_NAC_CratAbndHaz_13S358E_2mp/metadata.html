Description:
Abstract:
This is a crater abundance hazard mosaic of the Alphonsus Crater site generated from data files containing the percentage of area per hectare (100m x 100m) that is occupied by detected craters in the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) Images covering this site. The data files are created from crater lists computed from images acquired by the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Camera (LROC) NAC. Craters are modeled as ellipses. Craters of 2.5m on the major axis or larger can be fully resolved from the images. The smallest craters detected are approximately 2.0m on the major axis. Overlapping craters and craters inside craters are reported individually. The spatial distribution of craters is given in three different maps for each NAC image: a crater abundance data map, a crater abundance color (thematic) map, and a crater abundance hazard color (thematic) map. These are available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Alphonsus Crater, All. See also the crater density data products for each site.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The LRO Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Detected crater populations are useful to assist in the derivation of statistical models of crater density and abundance, and for joint analyses of multiple sources (e.g., DEM-derived local slope and roughness data). Crater lists generated from NAC images, and spatial maps derived from them, suggest surface crater populations of high density and abundance. Craters and boulders are hazardous to landers and rovers and their identification is thus essential for identifying potential landing site hazards, landed asset placement on the surface, surface traversal, and potentially ALHAT navigation.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
Projected crater locations and dimensions are obtained by applying the warping transform computed by registration of the non-mapped original NAC images onto the mapped NAC image mosaic. The registration method uses millions of points for correlation and works well provided the same NAC image used in crater detection was also used to generate the NAC Image Mosaic. When an overlapping, but different, image is used in the mosaic, the correlation has decreasing quality if the image illumination parameters vary compared to those of the non-mapped NAC image processed for crater detection.
Completeness_Report:
The Crater Abundance Hazard Mosaic only includes NAC images that were used for crater detection and used in the Image Mosaic for the site. The NAC Image used in crater detection must also exist in the Image Mosaic in order to georegister the image used in crater detection. In most cases, the number of NAC images where crater detection was run is signicantly larger than the number present in the Image Mosaic, resulting in a smaller than desired Crater Abundance Hazard Mosaic. The full set of all crater detection products for mapped and non-mapped NAC images is available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Alphonsus Crater, All.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy for this Crater Abundance Hazard Mosaic is the same as the horizontal accuracy of the Alphonsus Crater Image Mosaic. The horizontal accuracy measured is the RMS in the X (longitude) and Y (latitude) directions in meters of the final triangulation solution as reported in SOCET Set. The RMS values are X=16.542 and Y=7.3371. The overall RMS of the solution is 1.097 pixels. The pixel scale of the input images is 0.5m. The relative accuracy of the DEM is the same as the pixel scale, or 2.0 m. The images used in this project were controlled to LOLA tracks that were determined to be self-consistent, but which may not have been included in the crossover solution. The absolute accuracy of the crossover corrected LOLA data is 20 meters. The maximum horizontal offset for this DEM may be up to 36.5 m.

Process_Description:
This Crater Abundance Hazard Mosaic was created from fifteen data files containing the percentage area per hectare (100m x 100m) that is occupied by craters in the NAC Image (m111606491LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m111606491RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m113969390RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m129302602LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m129302602RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m129309387LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m129309387RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m135202732LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m135202732RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m135209517LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m135209517RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m137557200LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m137557200RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m137563985LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, and m137563985RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif). These data files were generated using crater lists for the following NAC images: m111606491LC, m111606491RC, m113969390RC, m129302602LC, m129302602RC, m129309387LC, m129309387RC, m135202732LC, m135202732RC, m135209517LC, m135209517RC, m137557200LC, m137557200RC, m137563985LC, and m137563985RC. The corner coordinates of each data file are tied to known locations and then the image is warped using GDAL. Then the set of mapped data products are mosaicked into a single image. The three colors represent levels of safety/risk due to crater abundance: green (0-10 percent, safe), yellow (11-30 percent, mild risk), graded red (31 percent or greater, increasing risk or unsafe). The represented risk is arbitrary but an educated guess from previous reports of crater freshness.
