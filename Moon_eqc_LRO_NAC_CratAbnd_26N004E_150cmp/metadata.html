Description:
Abstract:
This is a crater abundance mosaic of the Apollo 15 site generated from data files containing the percentage of area per hectare (100m x 100m) that is occupied by detected craters in the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) Images covering this site. The data files are created from crater lists computed from images acquired by the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Camera (LROC) NAC. Craters are modeled as ellipses. Craters of 2.5m on the major axis or larger can be fully resolved from the images. The smallest craters detected are approximately 2.0m on the major axis. Overlapping craters and craters inside craters are reported individually. The spatial distribution of craters is given in three different maps for each NAC image: a crater abundance data map, a crater abundance color (thematic) map, and a crater abundance hazard color (thematic) map. These are available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Apollo 15, All. See also the crater density data products for each site.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The LRO Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Detected crater populations are useful to assist in the derivation of statistical models of crater density and abundance, and for joint analyses of multiple sources (e.g., DEM-derived local slope and roughness data). Crater lists generated from NAC images, and spatial maps derived from them, suggest surface crater populations of high density and abundance. Craters and boulders are hazardous to landers and rovers and their identification is thus essential for identifying potential landing site hazards, landed asset placement on the surface, surface traversal, and potentially ALHAT navigation.

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
Projected crater locations and dimensions are obtained by applying the warping transform computed by registration of the non-mapped original NAC images onto the mapped NAC image mosaic. The registration method uses millions of points for correlation and works well provided the same NAC image used in crater detection was also used to generate the NAC Image Mosaic. When an overlapping, but different, image is used in the mosaic, the correlation has decreasing quality if the image illumination parameters vary compared to those of the non-mapped NAC image processed for crater detection.
Completeness_Report:
The Crater Abundace Mosaic only includes NAC images that were used for crater detection and used in the Image Mosaic for the site. The NAC Image used in crater detection must also exist in the Image Mosaic in order to georegister the image used in crater detection. In most cases, the number of NAC images where crater detection was run is signicantly larger than the number present in the Image Mosaic, resulting in a smaller than desired Crater Abundance Mosaic. The full set of all crater detection products for mapped and non-mapped NAC images is available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Apollo 15, All.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy for this Crater Abundance Mosaic is the same as the horizontal accuracy of the Apollo 15 Image Mosaic. For the original DEM used to control this mosaic the given horizontal accuracy is the RMS in the X (longitude) and Y (latitude) directions in meters of the final triangulation solution as reported in SOCET SET. The root mean squared (RMS) values are X=13.5m and Y=8.7m. The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is the same as the spatial resolution of the DEM, which is equivalent to the post spacing (typically 1.5m). The images used in this project were controlled to LOLA tracks that were determined to be self-consistent, but which may not have been included in a crossover solution. The total absolute error results from the combination of the DEM horizontal error and the LOLA track data horizontal error. The minimum absolute RMS accuracy of the crossover corrected LOLA data is estimated at 20m. Thus the maximum horizontal RMS for this DEM may be up to 24.1m.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
This Crater Abundance Mosaic was created from four data files containing the percentage area per hectare (100m x 100m) that is occupied by craters in the NAC Image (m129269398LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m129269398RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, m131623862LC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif, and m131623862RC_craterFAmap_100mp.tif). These data files were generated using crater lists for the following NAC images: m129269398LC, m129269398RC, m131623862LC, and m131623862RC. The corner coordinates of each data file are tied to known locations and then the image is warped using GDAL. Then the set of mapped data products are mosaicked into a single image. The five colors in the mosaic represent general purpose ranges of crater abundance: blue (0-10 percent), green (11-25 percent), yellow (26-40 percent), orange (41-50 percent) and graded red (51 percent or higher)
