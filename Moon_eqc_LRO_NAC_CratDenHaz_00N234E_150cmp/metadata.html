Description:
Abstract:
This is a crater density hazard mosaic of the Hertzsprung site generated from data files containing the number of detected craters (all sizes are included) per hectare (100m x 100m) in the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) Images covering this site. The data files are created from crater lists computed from images acquired by the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Camera (LROC) NAC. Craters are modeled as ellipses. Craters of 2.5m on the major axis or larger can be fully resolved from the images. The smallest craters detected are approximately 2.0m on the major axis. Overlapping craters and craters inside craters are reported individually. The spatial distribution of craters is given in three different maps for each NAC image: a crater density data map, a crater density color (thematic) map, and a crater density hazard color (thematic) map. These are available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Hertzsprung, All. See also the crater abundance data products for each site.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The LRO Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Detected crater populations are useful to assist in the derivation of statistical models of crater density and abundance, and for joint analyses of multiple sources (e.g., DEM-derived local slope and roughness data). Crater lists generated from NAC images, and spatial maps derived from them, suggest surface crater populations of high density and abundance. Craters and boulders are hazardous to landers and rovers and their identification is thus essential for identifying potential landing site hazards, landed asset placement on the surface, surface traversal, and potentially ALHAT navigation.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
Projected crater locations and dimensions are obtained by applying the warping transform computed by registration of the non-mapped original NAC images onto the mapped NAC image mosaic. The registration method uses millions of points for correlation and works well provided the same NAC image used in crater detection was also used to generate the NAC Image Mosaic. When an overlapping, but different, image is used in the mosaic, the correlation has decreasing quality if the image illumination parameters vary compared to those of the non-mapped NAC image processed for crater detection.
Completeness_Report:
The Crater Density Hazard Mosaic only includes NAC images that were used for crater detection and used in the Image Mosaic for the site. The NAC Image used in crater detection must also exist in the Image Mosaic in order to georegister the image used in crater detection. In most cases, the number of NAC images where crater detection was run is signicantly larger than the number present in the Image Mosaic, resulting in a smaller than desired Crater Density Hazard Mosaic. The full set of all crater detection products for mapped and non-mapped NAC images is available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Hertzsprung, All.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy for this Crater Density Hazard Mosaic is the same as the horizontal accuracy of the Hertzsprung Image Mosaic. For the original DEM used to control this mosaic the given horizontal accuracy is the RMS in the X (longitude) and Y (latitude) directions in meters of the final triangulation solution as reported in SOCET SET. The root mean squared (RMS) values are X=10.2m and Y=12.7m. The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is the same as the spatial resolution of the DEM, which is equivalent to the post spacing (typically 1.5m). The images used in this project were controlled to LOLA tracks that were determined to be self-consistent, but which may not have been included in a crossover solution. The total absolute error results from the combination of the DEM horizontal error and the LOLA track data horizontal error. The minimum absolute RMS accuracy of the crossover corrected LOLA data is estimated at 20m. Thus the maximum horizontal RMS for this DEM may be up to 23.7m.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
This Crater Density Hazard Mosaic was created from two data files containing the number of detected craters per hectare (100m x 100m) in the NAC Image (m118315693LC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif and m118315693RC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif). These data files were generated using crater lists for the following NAC images: m118315693LC and m118315693RC. The corner coordinates of each data file are tied to known locations and then the image is warped using GDAL. Then the set of mapped data products are mosaicked into a single image. The three colors in the mosaic represent levels of landing/traversal safety/risk due to crater density: green (0-10, safe), yellow (11-50, potentially hazardous), and graded red (51 or greater, hazardous). The represented risk is arbitrary but an educated guess from previous estimates of fresh craters (about 12 percent).
