Description:
Abstract:
This is a crater density mosaic of the Aristarchus 1 site generated from data files containing the number of detected craters (all sizes are included) per hectare (100m x 100m) in the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) Images covering this site. The data files are created from crater lists computed from images acquired by the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Camera (LROC) NAC. Craters are modeled as ellipses. Craters of 2.5m on the major axis or larger can be fully resolved from the images. The smallest craters detected are approximately 2.0m on the major axis. Overlapping craters and craters inside craters are reported individually. The spatial distribution of craters is given in three different maps for each NAC image: a crater density data map, a crater density color (thematic) map, and a crater density hazard color (thematic) map. These are available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Aristarchus 1, All. See also the crater abundance data products for each site.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The LRO Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Detected crater populations are useful to assist in the derivation of statistical models of crater density and abundance, and for joint analyses of multiple sources (e.g., DEM-derived local slope and roughness data). Crater lists generated from NAC images, and spatial maps derived from them, suggest surface crater populations of high density and abundance. Craters and boulders are hazardous to landers and rovers and their identification is thus essential for identifying potential landing site hazards, landed asset placement on the surface, surface traversal, and potentially ALHAT navigation.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
Projected crater locations and dimensions are obtained by applying the warping transform computed by registration of the non-mapped original NAC images onto the mapped NAC image mosaic. The registration method uses millions of points for correlation and works well provided the same NAC image used in crater detection was also used to generate the NAC Image Mosaic. When an overlapping, but different, image is used in the mosaic, the correlation has decreasing quality if the image illumination parameters vary compared to those of the non-mapped NAC image processed for crater detection.
Completeness_Report:
The Crater Density Mosaic only includes NAC images that were used for crater detection and used in the Image Mosaic for the site. The NAC Image used in crater detection must also exist in the Image Mosaic in order to georegister the image used in crater detection. In most cases, the number of NAC images where crater detection was run is signicantly larger than the number present in the Image Mosaic, resulting in a smaller than desired Crater Density Mosaic. The full set of all crater detection products for mapped and non-mapped NAC images is available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Aristarchus 1, All.

Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy for this Crater Density Mosaic is the same as the horizontal accuracy of the Aristarchus 1 Image Mosaic. To compute the absolute horizontal accuracy, three of the sixteen LOLA tracks crossing the DEM were selected at random for comparison. The data set that the LOLA tracks were chosen from contains tracks that were not crossover corrected. Multiple tracks were sampled to account for this and ensure that offsets were consistent. The DEM was registered to these tracks to calculate the horizontal offset relative to LOLA and therefore find the absolute horizontal offset. The average horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, is 7.5 meters. The maximum horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, was 19 meters. Since the accuracy of the LOLA data is 20 meters, the maximum absolute horizontal offset for this site is 39 meters. The orthophoto mosaic generated from this DEM will have the same accuracy
Quantitative_Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Assessment:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Value: 39
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is the same as the spatial resolution of the DEM which is 2m. The absolute horizontal accuracy is dependent upon the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data. When this DEM was made, the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data was 20 meters.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
This Crater Density Mosaic was created from eight data files containing the number of detected craters per hectare (100m x 100m) in the NAC Image (m120168714LC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m120168714RC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m120175500LC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m120175500RC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m122530208LC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m129608587LC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m129608587RC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif, m129615371LC_craterDENmap_100mp.tif). These data files were generated using crater lists for the following NAC images: m120168714LC, m120168714RC, m120175500LC, m120175500RC, m122530208LC, m129608587LC, m129608587RC, and m129615371LC. The corner coordinates of each data file are tied to known locations and then the image is warped using GDAL. Then the set of mapped data products are mosaicked into a single image. The five colors in the mosaic represent general purpose ranges of crater density: blue (0-25), green (26-45), yellow (46-65), orange (66-85) and graded red (86 or greater).
