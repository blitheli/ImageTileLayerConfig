Description:
Abstract:
This is an orthophoto mosaic of the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) stereo images used to create a digital elevation model (DEM) of the same area. Resolution of the original LROC NAC images and output mosaic was approximately 0.5m/pixel. The DEM was registered horizontally and vertically to the LRO Lunar Orbiter Laser Altimeter (LOLA) data. DEM values are in meters and referred to a radius of 1737400 meters. This image mosaic covers the Ina D-Caldera site.
Purpose:
The NASA Constellation Program (CxP) has identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon that the LROC NAC will image under various conditions. The DEMs are generated from this imagery. High resolution DEMs will be essential for identifying potential landing sites and landing site hazards, planning the placement of landed assets on the surface, as well as other surface operations and navigation. Site-specific DEMs will also serve as a foundation for ortho-projection and control of local/site mosaics.

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
For each DEM pixel, a derived confidence value is also assigned in the file named LRO_NAC_Conf_19N005E_2mp.tif. The confidence values used to assess the DEM pixels are: &gt; &gt; 0 = NoDATA, outside boundary &gt; 1 = Shadowed &gt; 2 = Saturated &gt; 3 = Suspicious (edge, corner, did not correlate, etc) &gt; 4 = Interpolated/Extrapolated from neighbor pixels &gt; 10-14 = Value range of successful correlations &gt; 15 = Manually interpolated
Completeness_Report:
Where the images contained stereo overlap, elevation values were extracted for the DEM except for when one of the images was in shadow.
Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
To compute the absolute horizontal accuracy, three of the sixteen LOLA tracks crossing the DEM were selected at random for comparison. The data set that the LOLA tracks were chosen from contains tracks that were not crossover corrected. Multiple tracks were sampled to account for this and ensure that offsets were consistent. The DEM was registered to these tracks to calculate the horizontal offset relative to LOLA and therefore find the absolute horizontal offset. The average horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, is 11.9 meters. The maximum horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, was 16.9 meters. Since the accuracy of the LOLA data is 20 meters, the maximum absolute horizontal offset for this site is 36.9 meters.
