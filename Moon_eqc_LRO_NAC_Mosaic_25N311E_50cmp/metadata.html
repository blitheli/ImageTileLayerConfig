Description:
Abstract:
This is an orthophoto mosaic of the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) stereo images used to create a digital elevation model (DEM) of the same area. Resolution of the original LROC NAC images and output mosaic was approximately 0.5m/pixel. The DEM was registered horizontally and vertically to the LRO Lunar Orbiter Laser Altimeter (LOLA) data. DEM values are in meters and referred to a radius of 1737400 meters. This image mosaic covers the Aristarchus site.
Purpose:
The NASA Constellation Program (CxP) has identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon that the LROC NAC will image under various conditions. The DEMs are generated from this imagery. High resolution DEMs will be essential for identifying potential landing sites and landing site hazards, planning the placement of landed assets on the surface, as well as other surface operations and navigation. Site-specific DEMs will also serve as a foundation for ortho-projection and control of local/site mosaics.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU). The nodata value for the mosaic is -32768

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
For each DEM pixel, a derived confidence value is also assigned in the file named LRO_NAC_Conf_25N311E_2mp.tif. The confidence values used to assess the DEM pixels are: &gt; &gt; 0 = NoDATA, outside boundary &gt; 1 = Shadowed &gt; 2 = Saturated &gt; 3 = Suspicious (edge, corner, did not correlate, etc) &gt; 4 = Interpolated/Extrapolated from neighbor pixels &gt; 10-14 = Value range of successful correlations &gt; 15 = Manually interpolated
Completeness_Report:
Where the images contained stereo overlap, elevation values were extracted for the DEM except for when one of the images was in shadow.
Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
To compute the absolute horizontal accuracy, three of the sixteen LOLA tracks crossing the DEM were selected at random for comparison. The data set that the LOLA tracks were chosen from contains tracks that were not crossover corrected. Multiple tracks were sampled to account for this and ensure that offsets were consistent. The DEM was registered to these tracks to calculate the horizontal offset relative to LOLA and therefore find the absolute horizontal offset. The average horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, is 7.5 meters. The maximum horizontal offset, relative to LOLA data, was 19 meters. Since the accuracy of the LOLA data is 20 meters, the maximum absolute horizontal offset for this site is 39 meters. The orthophoto mosaic generated from this DEM will have the same accuracy
Quantitative_Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Assessment:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Value: 39
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Explanation:
The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is the same as the spatial resolution of the DEM which is 2m. The absolute horizontal accuracy is dependent upon the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data. When this DEM was made, the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data was 20 meters.
