Description:
Abstract:
This is a rock density hazard mosaic of the Apollo Basin site generated from data files containing the number of detected rocks (all sizes are included) per hectare (100m x 100m) in the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC) Images covering this site. The data files are created from rock lists computed from images acquired by the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Camera (LROC) NAC. Rocks of 2.5m diameter or larger can be fully resolved from the images. The smallest rocks detected are approximately 1.0m in diameter. Clumped rocks may be reported as a single large rock when their shadows are merged. In addition to this mosaic, the spatial distribution of rocks is given in three different maps for each NAC image: a rock density data map, a rock density color (thematic) map, and a rock density hazard color (thematic) map. These are available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Apollo Basin, All. See also the rock density data products for each site.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The LRO Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Detected rock populations are useful to assist in the derivation of statistical models of rock density and abundance, and for joint analyses of multi-modal rock abundance estimation methods such as radar altimetry and thermal inertia studies. Rock lists generated from NAC images, and spatial maps derived from them, suggest surface rock populations near impact craters in greater abundance. Rocks and boulders are hazardous to landers and rovers and their identification is thus essential for identifying potential landing site hazards, landed asset placement on the surface, surface traversal, and potentially ALHAT navigation.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
Projected rock locations and dimensions are obtained by applying the warping transform computed by registration of the non-mapped original NAC images onto the mapped NAC image mosaic. The registration method uses millions of points for correlation and works well provided the same NAC image used in rock detection was also used to generate the NAC Image Mosaic. When an overlapping, but different, image is used in the mosaic, the correlation has decreasing quality if the image illumination parameters vary compared to those of the non-mapped NAC image processed for rock detection.
Completeness_Report:
The Rock Density Hazard Mosaic only includes NAC images that were used for rock detection and used in the Image Mosaic for the site. The NAC Image used in rock detection must also exist in the Image Mosaic in order to georegister the image used in rock detection. In most cases, the number of NAC images where rock detection was run is signicantly larger than the number present in the Image Mosaic, resulting in a smaller than desired Rock Density Hazard Mosaic. The full set of all rock detection products for mapped and non-mapped NAC images is available for download as part of the product LRO LROC Hazards, Apollo Basin, All.

Process_Description:
This Rock Density Hazard Mosaic was created from eighteen data files containing the number of detected rocks per hectare (100m x 100m) in the NAC Image (m107878265LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m107878265RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m110235527LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m110235527RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m112598394LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m123221325LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m125583467LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m125583467RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m127938184LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m127938184RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m127944969LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m127944969RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m130306447LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m130306447RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m133832543LC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m133832543RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, m138548216RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif, and m138554997RC_rockDENmap_100mp.tif). These data files were generated using rock lists for the following NAC images: m107878265LC, m107878265RC, m110235527LC, m110235527RC, m112598394LC, m123221325LC, m125583467LC, m125583467RC, m127938184LC, m127938184RC, m127944969LC, m127944969RC, m130306447LC, m130306447RC, m133832543LC, m133832543RC, m138548216RC, and m138554997RC. The corner coordinates of each data file are tied to known locations and then the image is warped using GDAL. Then the set of mapped data products are mosaicked into a single image. The three colors in the mosaic represent levels of landing/traversal safety/risk due to rock density: green (0-3, safe), yellow (4-10, potentially hazardous), and graded red (10 or greater, hazardous). The represented risk is arbitrary but an educated guess from previous Mars missions using similar rock detection technology.
