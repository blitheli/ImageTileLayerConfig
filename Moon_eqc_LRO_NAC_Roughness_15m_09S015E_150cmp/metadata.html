Description:
Abstract:
This is a local roughness map of the Digital Elevation Model (DEM) LRO_NAC_DEM_09S015E_150cmp.tif of Apollo 16, with a post spacing of 1.5 and a roughness baseline (scale) of 15m. Pixel values are in meters and the theoretical roughness uncertainty equivalent to that of the source DEM. See also the corresponding local roughness and roughness hazard color (thematic) maps.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Roughness data and thematic maps are derived from digital elevation models (DEM) and are essential for surface characterization and analysis, e.g., identifying potential landing sites, surface traversal planning, and landed asset placement.

Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy of the roughness map is the same as the DEM from which the roughness map was derived. The horizontal accuracy measured is the RMS in the X (longitude) and Y (latitude) directions in meters of the final triangulation solution as reported in SOCET Set. The relative horizontal accuracy (pixel to pixel across the DEM) is the same as the spatial resolution of the DEM, which is equivalent to the post spacing (typically 1.5 meters). The images used in this project were controlled to LOLA tracks that were determined to be self-consistent, and therefore the absolute horizontal accuracy is dependent on the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data. The total absolute error results from the combination of the DEM horizontal error and the LOLA track data horizontal error. The absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA track data could be as much as 300 meters, however, it is expected that the absolute horizontal accuracy of the LOLA data is or will be less than 20 meters after the crossover analysis is applied.

Vertical_Positional_Accuracy:
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute vertical accuracy of the roughness map is the same as the DEM from which the roughness map was derived. Since the DEM is tied to LOLA, the vertical accuracy is limited by both the absolute vertical accuracy of the LOLA data and the Expected Vertical Precision (the relative precision) of the DEM. The absolute vertical accuracy of the LOLA data is expected to approach 1 meter, but at the time this DEM was created, the vertical accuracy of the LOLA data was approximately 10 meters. The relative precision of the DEM is expected to be 1 meter, but can be as large as 2 meters.

1-10 meter absolute accuracy, 1-5 meter precision. Absolute accuracy is based on LOLA error estimates. Precision is based on SOCET SET precision and RMS error between LOLA profiles and the DEM.
