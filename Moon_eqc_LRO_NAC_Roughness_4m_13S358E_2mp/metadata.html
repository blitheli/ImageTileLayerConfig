Description:
Abstract:
This is a local roughness map derived from the Digital Elevation Model (DEM) LRO_NAC_DEM_13S358E_200cmp.tif of Alphonsus Crater, with a post spacing of 2m and a roughness baseline (scale) of 4m. Pixel values are in meters and the theoretical roughness uncertainty equivalent to that of the source DEM. See also the corresponding local roughness and roughness hazard color (thematic) maps.
Purpose:
The former Constellation Program (CxP) identified 50 regions of interest on the Moon. The Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) team has been imaging these regions under various conditions with the Narrow Angle Camera (NAC). Roughnessthe data and thematic maps are derived from digital elevation models (DEM) and are essential for surface characterization and analysis, e.g., identifying potential landing sites, surface traversal planning, and landed asset placement.
Supplemental_Information:
This product was created using West/East bounding coordinates of 0 to 360 degrees with longitudes increasing to the East. Due to FGDC standards, the bounding coordinates are reported here in the Spatial Domain section in terms of -180 to 180 degrees longitude. Both longitude methods are recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute horizontal accuracy of the roughness map is the same as the DEM from which the roughness map was derived. The horizontal accuracy measured is the RMS in the X (longitude) and Y (latitude) directions in meters of the final triangulation solution as reported in SOCET Set. The RMS values are X=16.542 and Y=7.3371. The overall RMS of the solution is 1.097 pixels. The pixel scale of the input images is 0.5m. The relative accuracy of the DEM is the same as the pixel scale, or 2.0 m. The images used in this project were controlled to LOLA tracks that were determined to be self-consistent, but which may not have been included in the crossover solution. The absolute accuracy of the crossover corrected LOLA data is 20 meters. The maximum horizontal offset for this DEM may be up to 36.5 m.

Vertical_Positional_Accuracy:
Vertical_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
The absolute vertical accuracy of the roughness map is the same as the DEM from which it is derived. The vertical accuracy measured in SOCET Set is the RMS Z=1.62323 m. The LOLA tracks used to control the images were compared to the DEM. The average difference between LOLA and the DEM is 2.5016 m with a standard deviation of 3.0233. The max error is 17.6401 m. The min error is 0.0002 m. The images used in this project were controlled to LOLA tracks that were determined to be self-consistent, but which may not have been included in the crossover solution. The estimated elevation accuracy for crossover corrected LOLA data is 1 meter. Therefore the average vertical accuracy offset for this DEM is 3.5 m with a maximum offset of up to 18.6 m.
