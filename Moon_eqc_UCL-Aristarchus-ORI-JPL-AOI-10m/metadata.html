Title: LRO NAC Ortho Mosaic, Aristarchus Crater

Abstract:
The NASA ARTEMIS Program is a human exploration mission carried out by NASA, US commercial spaceflight companies, ESA, JAXA, and CSA. The program is the next step in establishing a sustainable human presence on the moon amongst other goals. Aristarchus, a young crater with transient features on the Moon, was selected as a site for the Apollo 18 mission and is one of the landing site candidates of the ARTEMIS mission. In order to provide a VR dataset for astronaut training for the mission, the best possible 3D image dataset is needed. Using LROC-NAC stereo-pairs, this data has been constructed using stereo photogrammetry at the very high resolution of 0.5m/px for the orthorectified images (ORI) and a 1m/px Digital Terrain Model (DTM). The LROC-NAC instrument (Robinson et al., 2010) captures repeat pass single panchromatic grey-scale images at 0.5-2m/pixel over a swath-width of 2.5-10km with a limited number with suitable stereo angles.
For the NAC images included in the final ORI mosaic, each stereo-pair had one ORI selected and had similar solar angles (elevation and azimuth) to minimize the impact of shadows and to fill the gaps from the area where corresponding ORIs from DTMs are not available. Each image was then bundle-adjusted and co-aligned/co-registered using ASP. Individual ORIs intensity values are adjusted to roughly similar values with GDAL functions gdal_translate and gdal_calc. The images are orthorectified using the DTM mosaic and then mosaicked together with ASP function image_mosaic and GDAL function gdalwarp.
