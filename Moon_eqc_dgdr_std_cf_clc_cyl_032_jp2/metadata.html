Abstract:
Each sample represents an estimate of the Standard (STD) Christiansen feature (CF) position, which is the mid-infrared emissivity maximum near 8 microns for silicates. The CF is correlated with silicate polymerization providing compositional information. The wavelength position of CF is strongly dependent on the degree of polymerization of minerals, with framework silicate minerals such as feldspars exhibiting CFs at shorter wavelengths than less polymerized pyroxene and olivine. Common lunar minerals thus exhibit widely separated CF positions. The CF position of soils is close to the weighted average of the constituent minerals, which enables determination of major lunar lithologies.
